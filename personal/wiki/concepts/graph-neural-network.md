# Graph Neural Network

A [[Graph Neural Network]] (GNN) is a class of artificial neural networks designed to process data represented as graphs. By employing a "graph-in, graph-out" architecture, GNNs update embeddings for nodes, edges, and global contexts without altering the underlying graph connectivity. They rely on [[Message Passing Neural Network]] architectures to aggregate information from local neighborhoods while maintaining [[Permutation Invariance]].
