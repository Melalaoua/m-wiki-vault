# Ego Death in Competitive Play

[[ego-death-in-competitive-play]] is the practice of systematically stripping away outcome-oriented defensiveness to foster a pure growth mindset. \n\n
## Core Distinctions
- **Hating to Lose vs. Hating to Suck**: Hating to lose triggers emotional preservation (blaming teammates, switching champions, or deflecting to lag). Hating to suck focuses intensely on personal mistakes and choices, treating the discomfort of failure as a necessary marker for brain adaptation.
- **Release over Suppression**: Elite competitors do not suppress negative emotions; they allow them to exist fully, process them quickly, and release them, carrying no emotional baggage into subsequent matches.
- **Accepting the Discomfort**: Shifting away from ego-driven play requires embracing the painful reality that a defeat or misplay was entirely within one's control.
