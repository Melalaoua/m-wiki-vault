# Paul Graham's Startup and Work Philosophy

[[Paul Graham]] is an essayist, programmer, and investor whose writing extensively covers startup culture, intellectual independence, and career strategy. In 2005, he co-founded [[Y Combinator]] alongside Jessica Livingston. This organization operates as a hybrid investment firm and educational program for startup founders, funding approximately 6,500 companies by 2026 while emphasizing deep user empathy and monthly growth rates. Graham's principles on building companies emphasize [[Organic Startup Idea Generation]], asserting that the most effective startup concepts emerge not from intentional hunting, which often yields overly conservative ideas, but from informal side projects built with peers to solve personal needs. This philosophy parallels his framework for achieving [[Great Work]], defined as the ambitious output at the intersection of natural aptitude and deep interest. The process involves choosing a field, reaching its frontier of knowledge, noticing anomalies, and bravely exploring those gaps through continuous iteration, cultivating curiosity, and managing morale.
