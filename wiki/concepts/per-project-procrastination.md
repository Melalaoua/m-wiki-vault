# Per-Project Procrastination

[[wiki/concepts/per-project-procrastination]] is the tendency to delay the start of large, ambitious projects, often for years. It is much more dangerous than daily procrastination because it frequently camouflages itself as "industrious work." You feel busy working on smaller, less important tasks, so the alarm bells of procrastination never go off. Beating it requires periodically stopping to ask: "Am I working on what I most want to work on?"

Source: [[wiki/sources/paulgraham2026great|Great Work]]

## From [[wiki/sources/notes202633cod|33-COD]] (2026-07-08)

Procrastination often stalls momentum on specific projects. According to [[The Cult of Done]], one effective remedy is enforcing a strict timeline on nascent ideas: 'If you wait more than a week to get an idea done, abandon it.' The goal is to avoid getting bogged down trying to force a stuck idea, and instead move on to the next one to maintain creative flow.
