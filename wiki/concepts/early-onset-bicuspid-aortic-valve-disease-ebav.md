# Early-Onset Bicuspid Aortic Valve Disease (EBAV)

[[wiki/concepts/early-onset-bicuspid-aortic-valve-disease|Early-Onset Bicuspid Aortic Valve Disease (EBAV)]] is a clinical phenotype characterized by individuals with [[wiki/concepts/bicuspid-aortic-valve|BAV]] who develop significant complications before the age of 30. As defined in a 2026 study by Mansoorshahi et al., this includes the presentation of moderate or severe aortic valve stenosis or regurgitation, a large [[wiki/concepts/thoracic-aortic-aneurysm|thoracic aortic aneurysm]] (Z score > 4), or the need for aortic valve/aortic surgery or intervention. This early and severe disease course suggests a stronger underlying genetic component, potentially involving rare variants in genes like [[wiki/entities/col1a1|COL1A1]] and [[wiki/entities/col1a2|COL1A2]] that act as disease modifiers.
