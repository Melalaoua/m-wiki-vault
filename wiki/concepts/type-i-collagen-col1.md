# Type I Collagen (COL1)

[[wiki/concepts/type-i-collagen|Type I collagen (COL1)]] is the most abundant protein in the human body, providing essential mechanical strength and scaffolding for connective tissues such as bone, skin, tendons, and arteries. It is a heterotrimer composed of two alpha-1 chains and one alpha-2 chain, encoded by the [[wiki/entities/col1a1|COL1A1]] and [[wiki/entities/col1a2|COL1A2]] genes, respectively. Pathogenic variants in these genes are known to cause disorders like [[wiki/concepts/osteogenesis-imperfecta|Osteogenesis Imperfecta]] and [[wiki/concepts/ehlers-danlos-syndrome|Ehlers-Danlos Syndrome]]. Recent research has also implicated rare variants in these genes as risk modifiers for [[wiki/concepts/early-onset-bicuspid-aortic-valve-disease|early-onset bicuspid aortic valve disease]].
