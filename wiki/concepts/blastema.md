# Blastema

A blastema is a mass of cells capable of growth and [[wiki/concepts/regeneration]] into organs or body parts. In mammals, these structures are typically absent, replaced by [[wiki/concepts/fibrosis]] and scar tissue formation via [[wiki/concepts/fibroblast]] cells. Recent research has shown that blastema-like structures can be induced in mammals using growth factors such as [[wiki/concepts/fibroblast-growth-factor-2|FGF2]], shifting cellular behavior away from scarring.
