# Bicuspid Aortic Valve (BAV)

[[wiki/concepts/bicuspid-aortic-valve|Bicuspid Aortic Valve (BAV)]] is the most common congenital heart malformation, affecting approximately 1% of the population. Instead of the normal three leaflets (tricuspid), the aortic valve has only two. This condition often follows an autosomal dominant inheritance pattern with incomplete penetrance. BAV is associated with a significantly increased lifetime risk of developing [[wiki/concepts/thoracic-aortic-aneurysm|thoracic aortic aneurysms (TAAs)]], aortic dissection, and valvular heart disease (such as aortic stenosis or regurgitation) requiring surgical intervention. While complications typically manifest after age 40, a subset of individuals experience severe, [[wiki/concepts/early-onset-bicuspid-aortic-valve-disease|early-onset complications (EBAV)]].
