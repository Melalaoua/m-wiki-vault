# Positional re-specification

Positional re-specification is a biological process where cells are redirected to create structures outside their usual location or instructed to rebuild a different structure following an injury. It is a vital mechanism in [[wiki/concepts/regeneration]] and developmental biology, challenging assumptions that certain mammalian cells are entirely unprogrammable.
