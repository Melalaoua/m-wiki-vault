# Trio-Exome Sequencing

[[Trio-exome-sequencing]] is a genomic analysis technique where the [[exome]] (the protein-coding regions of the [[genome]]) of an affected individual (proband) and their two biological parents are sequenced simultaneously. This approach is particularly powerful for identifying the genetic cause of rare diseases.

By comparing the child's [[DNA]] to the parents', researchers can efficiently identify *de novo* mutations (variants present in the child but not in either parent), which are a common cause of severe congenital disorders. It also helps clarify the inheritance patterns of recessive and dominant variants.

In the context of prenatal diagnosis, as demonstrated in the fgene-17-1761449 study, trio-ES can provide a definitive diagnosis in a significant number of cases with fetal anomalies. The study found an overall [[diagnostic-yield]] of 28% for selected prenatal indications, with one-third of these diagnoses being missed by more narrow, targeted gene panels.
