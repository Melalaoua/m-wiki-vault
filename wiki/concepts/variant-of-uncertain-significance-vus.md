# Variant of Uncertain Significance (VUS)

A [[variant-of-uncertain-significance]] (VUS) is a genetic alteration identified through [[DNA]] sequencing whose association with disease risk is not yet established. According to the [[American-college-of-medical-genetics]] (ACMG) guidelines, these are classified as 'class 3' variants.

A VUS is identified when there is insufficient or conflicting evidence to classify a variant as either pathogenic (disease-causing) or benign (harmless). The interpretation can be challenging and may change over time as more scientific evidence becomes available.

In the context of prenatal diagnosis, VUSs present a significant challenge. The fgene-17-1761449 study identified VUSs in 11.6% of cases. The limited time frame of pregnancy makes it difficult to resolve the significance of these variants, complicating genetic counseling and parental decision-making. French guidelines at the time of the study did not permit the disclosure of VUSs before birth.
