# Diagnostic Yield

In medical genetics, [[diagnostic-yield]] refers to the percentage of patients with a particular clinical presentation who receive a definitive molecular diagnosis after a specific genetic test. It is a key metric for evaluating the effectiveness and clinical utility of a diagnostic test.

The fgene-17-1761449 study evaluated the diagnostic yield of [[trio-exome-sequencing]] for fetuses with specific abnormalities seen on ultrasound. The overall diagnostic yield for pathogenic or likely pathogenic variants was 28% (24 out of 86 fetuses). The study also highlighted that the yield is highly dependent on the specific clinical indication:
- **Vermian hypoplasia:** 42%
- **Polymalformative syndromes:** 30%
- **Large hyperechogenic kidneys:** 24%
- **Agenesis of corpus callosum (ACC):** 20%

This demonstrates that while a test may have a certain overall yield, its effectiveness can vary significantly for different subgroups of patients.
