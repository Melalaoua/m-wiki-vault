# Ciliopathy

A [[ciliopathy]] is a class of genetic disorders caused by mutations in genes that encode proteins essential for the formation or function of primary cilia. Cilia are microtubule-based organelles found on the surface of most vertebrate cells, acting as cellular antennae that play critical roles in various signaling pathways.

Due to the widespread presence and diverse functions of cilia, ciliopathies can affect multiple organ systems, leading to a wide range of clinical features, including:
- Polycystic kidney disease
- Retinal degeneration
- Brain malformations (e.g., Joubert syndrome)
- Skeletal abnormalities (e.g., polydactyly)
- Liver fibrosis

The fgene-17-1761449 study found that variants in ciliopathy genes were a major cause of fetal anomalies, accounting for 18% of identified genetic causes. They were particularly prevalent in fetuses presenting with large hyperechogenic kidneys, with variants found in genes like [[PKD1]], [[BBS1]], [[HNF1B]], and [[CEP290]].
