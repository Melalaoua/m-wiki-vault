# COL1A2

The [[wiki/entities/col1a2|COL1A2]] gene provides instructions for making the pro-alpha2 chain of [[wiki/concepts/type-i-collagen|type I collagen]]. This chain combines with two alpha1 chains (from the [[wiki/entities/col1a1|COL1A1]] gene) to form the type I procollagen molecule. Pathogenic variants in [[wiki/entities/col1a2|COL1A2]] are known to cause [[wiki/concepts/osteogenesis-imperfecta|Osteogenesis Imperfecta (OI)]] and certain subtypes of [[wiki/concepts/ehlers-danlos-syndrome|Ehlers-Danlos Syndrome]], including the rare cardiac-valvular type (EDSCV). Rare variants in [[wiki/entities/col1a2|COL1A2]] have also been identified as risk modifiers in [[wiki/concepts/early-onset-bicuspid-aortic-valve-disease|early-onset bicuspid aortic valve disease]].
