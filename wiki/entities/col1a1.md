# COL1A1

The [[wiki/entities/col1a1|COL1A1]] gene provides instructions for making the pro-alpha1 chain of [[wiki/concepts/type-i-collagen|type I collagen]]. Two alpha1 chains combine with one alpha2 chain (from the [[wiki/entities/col1a2|COL1A2]] gene) to form the type I procollagen molecule. Pathogenic variants in [[wiki/entities/col1a1|COL1A1]] are a primary cause of [[wiki/concepts/osteogenesis-imperfecta|Osteogenesis Imperfecta (OI)]] and some forms of [[wiki/concepts/ehlers-danlos-syndrome|Ehlers-Danlos Syndrome (EDS)]]. Studies have also linked rare variants in this gene to an increased risk for severe, [[wiki/concepts/early-onset-bicuspid-aortic-valve-disease|early-onset complications]] in individuals with [[wiki/concepts/bicuspid-aortic-valve|bicuspid aortic valve]].
