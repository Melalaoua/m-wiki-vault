# RHOA gene

The [[RHOA]] (Ras homolog family member A) gene provides instructions for making the RhoA protein, which is part of the Rho family of small GTPases. The RhoA protein is a key regulator of the actin [[cytoskeleton]], playing a crucial role in cell shape, migration, and division.

Pathogenic variants in the [[RHOA]] gene are associated with a rare condition characterized by cerebral, ocular, and acral (limb) anomalies with ectodermal dysplasia (MIM #618727). In the fgene-17-1761449 study, a fetus with moderately enlarged ventricles and unilateral anophthalmia was found to have a *de novo* somatic mosaic variant (p.Glu47Lys) in the [[RHOA]] gene. The variant was present in 13% of the cells from the amniotic fluid sample. This finding was consistent with the post-mortem examination, which revealed unilateral anophthalmia and syndactyly, supporting the molecular diagnosis.
