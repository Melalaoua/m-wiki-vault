# Rare type I collagen variants in early-onset bicuspid aortic valve disease: Overlap with Ehlers-Danlos syndrome and osteogenesis imperfecta

Original: [[raw/phd/PIIS2666247726000679.pdf]]

This study investigates the genetic underpinnings of early-onset bicuspid aortic valve (EBAV) disease, defined as individuals experiencing significant valve or aortic complications before age 30. [[wiki/concepts/bicuspid-aortic-valve|Bicuspid aortic valve (BAV)]] is the most common congenital heart defect and is often associated with [[wiki/concepts/thoracic-aortic-aneurysm|thoracic aortic aneurysms]] and aortic stenosis.

### Summary
The research team performed [[wiki/concepts/whole-exome-sequencing|whole-exome sequencing]] on 272 non-syndromic EBAV probands and their relatives. They identified rare, predicted damaging variants in the [[wiki/entities/col1a1|COL1A1]] and [[wiki/entities/col1a2|COL1A2]] genes, which encode [[wiki/concepts/type-i-collagen|type I collagen]], in 10 probands (4%). This represents a 30-fold enrichment compared to control populations. These individuals showed subtle features overlapping with [[wiki/concepts/osteogenesis-imperfecta|Osteogenesis Imperfecta (OI)]] and [[wiki/concepts/ehlers-danlos-syndrome|Ehlers-Danlos Syndrome (EDS)]], such as joint hypermobility and recurrent fractures. The variants found in the EBAV cohort were different from the glycine substitutions typically seen in severe OI.

### Background
[[wiki/concepts/type-i-collagen|Type I collagen]] is a crucial protein for the structural integrity of bone, skin, tendons, and arteries. Pathogenic variants in [[wiki/entities/col1a1|COL1A1]] and [[wiki/entities/col1a2|COL1A2]] are known to cause [[wiki/concepts/osteogenesis-imperfecta|OI]] (a bone fragility disorder) and several subtypes of [[wiki/concepts/ehlers-danlos-syndrome|EDS]] (connective tissue disorders). While cardiovascular issues have been noted in OI cohorts, these genes had not previously been implicated in sporadic BAV. The researchers hypothesized that EBAV represents a phenotypic extreme enriched for rare genetic variants with significant biological effects.

### Methods
The study enrolled participants from the Genetic Risks for Bicuspid Aortic Valve Disease study. EBAV was defined as developing moderate/severe aortic valve stenosis/regurgitation, a large thoracic aortic aneurysm (TAA), or requiring aortic surgery before age 30. Whole-exome sequencing was performed, and variants were filtered based on frequency, inheritance pattern, and predicted damage. Participants with identified COL1 variants were recontacted to assess for clinical features of OI and EDS.

### Results
Rare missense variants in [[wiki/entities/col1a1|COL1A1]] (5 probands) and [[wiki/entities/col1a2|COL1A2]] (5 probands) were identified in 10 of the 272 EBAV probands. Unlike typical OI-causing variants, only two of these were glycine substitutions in the triple-helical domain. Phenotypic analysis of the recontacted individuals revealed features like fractures from minor trauma, bone deformities, scoliosis, dentinogenesis imperfecta, and joint hypermobility. Family segregation analysis suggested the COL1 variants were insufficient to cause BAV on their own, supporting their role as modifier alleles that influence disease severity.

### Discussion
The findings suggest that rare, deleterious COL1 variants contribute to the EBAV phenotype. These variants appear to create an overlap syndrome with features of OI and EDS, but without the severe classic presentations. The authors propose that these variants disrupt collagen assembly and reduce the tissue's mechanical integrity, making the aortic valve and wall more susceptible to hemodynamic stress and accelerating disease progression. This study implicates [[wiki/entities/col1a1|COL1A1]] and [[wiki/entities/col1a2|COL1A2]] as significant risk genes for EBAV, potentially useful for future clinical risk stratification.

## Key claims

- Rare coding variants in type I collagen genes (COL1A1 and COL1A2) were found in 4% of early-onset bicuspid aortic valve (EBAV) disease probands, a 30-fold enrichment over control populations. ([[raw/phd/PIIS2666247726000679.pdf#page=1]]) — "Rare coding variants in COL1A1 (n = 5) and COL1A2 (n = 5) were identified in 10 (4%) EBAV probands, representing a 30-fold enrichment compared with European ancestry populations."
- EBAV is defined by the presentation of moderate to severe aortic valve or aortic complications before the age of 30. ([[raw/phd/PIIS2666247726000679.pdf#page=2]]) — "We defined early-onset complications of BAV (EBAV) as clinical presentation due to moderate or severe aortic valve stenosis or regurgitation or a large TAA (Z score > 4), or aortic valve or aortic surgery, aortic dissection, or intervention for other congenital left-sided lesions prior to age 30."
- Unlike the collagen variants that typically cause Osteogenesis Imperfecta (OI), which are glycine substitutions, only two of the variants found in EBAV patients were of this type. ([[raw/phd/PIIS2666247726000679.pdf#page=4]]) — "While all OI missense variants were glycine substitutions in the triple-helical domains, only two EBAV-associated variants were glycine substitutions affecting COL1A2..."
- The studied EBAV individuals with rare COL1 variants displayed some features of OI and EDS but lacked the severe, classic phenotypes of those disorders. ([[raw/phd/PIIS2666247726000679.pdf#page=5]]) — "EBAV participants harboring rare COL1 variants presented with variable features of OI and EDS but did not exhibit severe OI phenotypes such as blue sclerae, unusual fractures or bone deformities, fragile dentition, or hearing loss."
- The study suggests that COL1 variants act as modifier alleles that increase the severity of BAV-related disease rather than being the sole cause of BAV. ([[raw/phd/PIIS2666247726000679.pdf#page=5]]) — "Collectively, these observations indicate that COL1 variants are insufficient to cause BAV in isolation. Rather, they appear to act as modifier alleles, influencing the severity of valvular and aortic phenotypes and potentially accelerating the clinical recognition of BAV-related disease."
