# Mammalian Blastema Induction

In mammals, tissue injury typically results in fibrosis and scar tissue formation mediated by fibroblast cells, rather than true regeneration. However, research led by [[Ken Muneoka]] at the Texas A&M College of Veterinary Medicine and Biomedical Sciences focuses on overcoming these limitations. His work demonstrates that applying specific growth factors, notably [[Fibroblast Growth Factor 2 (FGF2)]] alongside bone morphogenetic protein 2 (BMP2), to healed wounds can redirect fibroblast cellular behavior away from scarring. This intervention successfully induces a [[Blastema]]—a mass of cells usually absent in mammals but inherently capable of robust growth and regeneration into distinct organs or body parts.
