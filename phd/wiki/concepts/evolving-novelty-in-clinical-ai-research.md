# Evolving Novelty in Clinical AI Research

The user (speeps/Medhi), having transitioned from an MD to a PhD, recognized that simply applying generative AI (like LLMs) to clinical diagnostics has become saturated and lost its research novelty. This realization—while initially triggering impostor syndrome and a sense of being lost—drove a strategic pivot toward [[biological-world-models-via-jepa]] and [[thesis-topic-shift-world-models-vs-llms]]. 

This shift marks a critical maturation in the [[outer-loop-of-research]]: recognizing when a field has become 'bland' and strategically identifying an emerging frontier (World Models) to maintain real-world utility and novelty. The drive to remain 'useful and fresh' builds on their proven track record of clinical utility, such as the [[diagnostic-tool-for-febrile-children-in-car|OAD project]].
