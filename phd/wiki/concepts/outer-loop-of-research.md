# Outer Loop of Research

The "Outer Loop" of research refers to the meta-level problem space in academia. While the inner loop consists of executing predefined solutions and running experiments, the outer loop is about figuring out *what* problems are worth solving and ripe for exploration. Developing a refined sense of taste to operate successfully in this loop is a primary goal of a [[phd/wiki/maps/phd|PhD]].
