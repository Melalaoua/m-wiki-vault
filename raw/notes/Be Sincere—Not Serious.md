### Summary of Relevant Advice:

1. **Attitude Over Workload:**
   - The state of enjoyment or stress at work is often influenced more by personal attitude than by the workload itself. Shifting one's attitude can make challenging tasks feel more enjoyable.

2. **Sincerity vs. Seriousness:**
   - Strive to be sincere rather than serious. Sincerity involves a curious and playful approach to challenges, while seriousness often leads to tension and stress.

3. **Life as a Ride or Game:**
   - Life can be seen as a ride or a game, where being too serious can make it less enjoyable. Recognize the playful nature of life and don’t get too caught up in its challenges.

4. **Finite vs. Infinite Games:**
   - Differentiate between finite games (short-term goals with winners and losers) and infinite games (long-term pursuits aimed at continuous play). Prioritize the infinite game of enjoying your career or relationships over merely "winning" short-term goals.

5. **Life as a Dance:**
   - Treat life more like a dance rather than a journey with a serious end goal. Focus on enjoying the process and the present moment rather than just the outcome.

6. **Performance Through Playfulness:**
   - Less tightly gripping life’s challenges can lead to better performance. Embrace a playful attitude to unlock better results with less effort.

7. **Constructive Playfulness:**
   - In professional settings, adopting a playful attitude can lead to more effective outcomes. Don’t be afraid to incorporate humor or light-heartedness to ease tension and improve collaboration.

8. **Starting Small:**
   - Begin by applying these principles in your personal life before tackling more challenging professional contexts. Notice areas of tightness and seriousness and see if you can infuse them with enjoyment.

9. **Exaggerate Seriousness:**
   - Consciously exaggerate your seriousness to see if it helps you find levity. This can shift your perspective and help you recognize the absurdity of excessive seriousness.

10. **Perspective Through Mortality:**
    - Practicing memento mori, or the meditation on one’s own death, can help maintain perspective. Remembering that life is finite can reduce undue seriousness and help you focus on enjoying the present.
