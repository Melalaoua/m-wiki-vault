I'm starting to cope better as a professional imposter, recognising I'm only really happy working right on the very edge of what I know and what I don't know. For twenty years I've chased job roles and projects as vehicles for learning, aiming to hone our design craft at the state of the art. But now I realise there's not treshold of achievements in craft that quiets the imposter in your head.. you can only get better at working with that voice.
