Ernest Hemingway, one of the most influential writers of the 20th century, is renowned for his novels like _For Whom the Bell Tolls_ and The _Old Man and the Sea_. In addition to his remarkable literary achievements, he excelled as a journalist and war correspondent. Hemingway’s unique writing style, characterized by its simplicity and conciseness, left a lasting impact on a whole generation of writers.

Hemingway was born on July 21, 1899, in Oak Park, Illinois. In high school, he co-edited the school newspaper, competed in football and swimming, and enjoyed impromptu boxing matches with friends. He also played the cello in the school orchestra.
