
### Module 1 : Approaches to scientific monitoring

#### Principles of science
- Science is a common : should be shared with the whole scientific community
- Universalism is the idea if someone else were to do the same research, they should get the same results
- Disinterestedness : distrust/mistrust of personal interests
- Peer reviewing
- Originality : being the first who produced a new result
- Humility : stay humble


Workshop : 
- Universalism : A defined framework is important 
- Originality : Keep a tab on the latests papers and stay in the loop.


1.  Definition of objectives and subject : often overlooked. Have an impact on the next steps (important). Chunking your subject in smaller parts leads to better scientific monitoring. What are the things i needs from the bibliography ?
2. Search for sources (using keywords/ using fundamental documents).
3. Sort relevant informations in the article from step 2
4. Synthesis.

The processus is non-linear, loop from 4 to 2. Look at the frontier. On the other hand, don't get lost too long.
Also sort the irrelevant informations


Workshop : Problem issues with monitoring and bibliography :
- Inconsistent : Not a continous. I want a bibliographic system to be regular, daily, weekly. To stay in the loop and consistent with my ongoing research.

- Superficial : The methodology is too linear, based on serenpidity instead of actual strategy. The writing/notes are not complete, too thin.



- Active monitoring : active search for information on a subject (pull logic) : State of the art at the beginning of the project. Themtic modifications of the project.

- Continous monitoring : sources are automatically identified (push logic).

Quick reading of a paper : get a first impression of the document to decide on its relevance
- Most relevant items first : title, abstract, figures ,section
- Check if other sources mention this document and what they say about it
- Retraced paper


Indepth reading : analyze and synthetize
- What are the key points ? What are the limits (ethical, methodological, range) ? WHat is the link with your work ?
- Before reading a section : what do you think it will be about ?

- Imponderable events prevents you from reading on scheduled time : move the slot rather than cancel it
