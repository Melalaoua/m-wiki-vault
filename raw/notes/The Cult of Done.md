### I. Three states of being 

1. Not knowing : Stade initial - Tu ne connais pas, tu n'as aucune connaissance sur un problème, une tâche, n'importe quelle situation. Ce stage est caractérisé par l'ignorance, la [[Words of Wisdom 07-07-2023|curiosity]], l'incertitude
2.  Action : C'est ici que tu prends les mesures nécessaires pour apprendre, découvrir, t'instruire. Cette étape implique l'effort, la pratique, et presque toujours, l'erreur.
3. Completion : Stade final - La tâche/situation est en phase de résolution/complétion. L'étape d'ignorance est transformée en connaissance au travers de l'action

Ces 3 étapes sont cycliques et continues. Chaque [[Reinforcing feedback loop|itération permet d'aller plus loin]].



### II. Accepte que tout n'est que brouillon.
Les [[Meeps|prototypes deviennent produit]], de [[Loi de Gall|simples esquisses deviennent des plans long termes]], les [[The Extended Mind|choses que tu écris pour les sortir de ta tête]] sont in fine écris dans un livre publié. 

Ce que tu fais n'est qu'un brouillon, ca na pas besoin d'être parfait, ca ne le seras jamais, même après que ce soit fini.

```ad-quote
I'm doing a (free) operating system (just a hobby, won't be big and professional like gnu)
-Linus Torvalds, 21 ans, changeant le monde.
```


### III. Pas de modification possible.
Painting has no editing, if you make a mistake YOU START AGAIN.
Performing music has no editing, if you make a mistake you keep going and hope the audience didn't notice.
And Pottery has no editing, if it comes out of the kiln and you don't like it, smash it to pieces and start again.

```ad-quote
I call it my billion-dollar mistake. It was the invention of the null reference in 1965.
-Tony Hoare
```

This principle models the way the world actually works.
When you release something into the world, be it a book or a program language, you've lost control of it, in a very big way.

Learn to accept this. Don't tweak what you've got, make another one.

### Fake it 'till you make it
Pretrendre que tu sais ce que tu fais est presque pareil que de savoir ce que tu es en train de faire.


### Bannis la procrastination
Si une idée reste en attente plus d'une semaine : discard la, transforme la.

When creativity strikes, it flows out of your fingers into your keys, or your paintbrush, or your guitar.

But sometimes the muses don't sing.



### Quand tu as finis tu peux t'en débarasser.
L'art n'est pas l'oeuvre,
L'art ne seras jamais l'oeuvre,
L'art est la chose qui a lieu en toi quand tu réalise l'oeuvre, ce sentiment d'euphorie, d'excitation en toi, d'épanouissement.

```ad-important
There is a vitality, Martha says, a life force, a quickening that is translated through you into action, and there is only one of you in all time, this expression is unique, and if you block it, it will never exist through any other medium; and be lost. The world will not have it.

It is not your business to determine how good it is, not how it compares with other expression. It is your business to keep it yours clearly and directly, to ==keep the channel open==. You do not even have to believe in yourself or your work. ==You have to keep open and aware directly to the urges that motivate you. Keep the channel open.==

No artist is pleased. There is no satisfaction whatever at any time. There is only a queer, divine dissatisfaction, a blessed unrest that keeps us marching and makes us more alive than the others.
```


### [[Failure Paradox|Failure]] is **good.**
L'echec est une bonne chose. Si tu n'essaie pas tu ne peux pas échouer. L'echec te montre que <u>tu as essayé</u>. Tu sauras quoi ne PAS faire la prochaine fois.


### THE Cult Of $DONE$

::: block <!-- element style="font-size: 0.6em;" -->
1.   There are three states of being.
     Not knowing, action and completion.
2.   Accept that everything is a draft. It helps to get it $done$.
3.   There is no editing stage.
4.   Pretending you know what you’re doing is almost the same as knowing what you are doing, so just accept that you know what you’re doing even if you don’t and do it.
5.   Banish procrastination. If you wait more than a week to get an idea  done, abandon it.
6.   The point of being  done is not to finish but to get other things $done$.
7.   Once you’re $done$ you can throw it away.
8.   Laugh at perfection. It’s boring and keeps you from being $done$.
9.   People without dirty hands are wrong. Doing something makes you right.
10.  Failure counts as $done$. So do mistakes.
11.  Destruction is a variant of $done$.
12.  If you have an idea and publish it on the internet, that counts as a ghost of $done$.
13.  $done$ is the engine of more.
