In the past decade, sequencing technology has evolved rapidly with the advent of high-throughput next-generation sequencing. By adopting and leveraging next-generation sequencing, clinical laboratories are now performing an ever-increasing catalogue of genetic testing spanning genotyping, single genes, gene panels, exomes, genomes, transcriptomes, and epigenetic assays for genetic disorders. By virtue of increased complexity, this shift in genetic testing has been accompanied by new challenges in sequence interpretation. In this context the ACMG convened a workgroup in 2013 comprising representatives from the ACMG, the Association for Molecular Pathology (AMP), and the College of American Pathologists to revisit and revise the standards and guidelines for the interpretation of sequence variants. The group consisted of clinical laboratory directors and clinicians. This report represents expert opinion of the workgroup with input from ACMG, AMP, and College of American Pathologists stakeholders. These recommendations primarily apply to the breadth of genetic tests used in clinical laboratories, including genotyping, single genes, panels, exomes, and genomes. This report recommends the use of specific standard terminology—“pathogenic,” “likely pathogenic,” “uncertain significance,” “likely benign,” and “benign”—to describe variants identified in genes that cause Mendelian disorders. Moreover, this recommendation describes a process for classifying variants into these five categories based on criteria using typical types of variant evidence (e.g., population data, computational data, functional data, segregation data). Because of the increased complexity of analysis and interpretation of clinical genetic testing described in this report, the ACMG strongly recommends that clinical molecular genetic testing should be performed in a Clinical Laboratory Improvement Amendments–approved laboratory, with results interpreted by a board-certified clinical molecular geneticist or molecular genetic pathologist or the equivalent.

## Notes

A mutation is defined as a permanent change in the nucleotide sequence, wheareas a polymorphism is defined as a variant with a frequency above 1%. The terms 'mutations' and 'polymorphism', however, which have been used widely, often lead to confusion because of incorrect assumptions of pathogenic and benign effects, respectively. Thus, it is recommended that both terms be replaced by the term "variant" with the following modifiers :
- (i) ==pathogenic==.
- (ii) ==likely pathogenic==.
- (iii) ==uncertain signifiance==.
- (iv) ==likely benign==.
- (v) ==benign==.

It is recommendend that all assertions of pathogenicity be reported with respect to a condition and inheritence pattern.

## Liste des bases de données aidant à la détermination de la pathogénicité d'un variant.

### Population database.
- ==Exome Aggregation Consortium== : Base de données de variants trouvés durant le séquençage d'exome de 61, 486 individus dans le cadre de l'étude de diverses maladies génétiques. 
- ==Exome variant Server== : Base de données de variants découvert durant le séquençage de plusieurs cohortes d'individus Européens, Afro-américains.
- ==1000 genomes project== : Base de données de variants découvert dans le séquençage (haute et faible couverture) de 26 populations. Plus haute diversité que l'exome variant server, mais des données de plus basse qualité, certaines cohortes comportent des individus apparentés.
- ==dbSNP== : Base de données de SNV soumis par diverses sources. Manque de détails sur la provenance des données, peut contenir des variants pathogènes.
- ==dbVAR== : Base de données de SV soumis par diverses sources.

### Disease databases.
- ==ClinVar== : Base de données de diagnostics cliniques et phénotypes en lien avec des variants.
- ==OMIM== : Base de données de gènes humains. Contiens aussi des variants génétiques en lien avec des maladies.
- ==Humane Gene Mutation Database== : Base de données de variant annotés publiés dans la litérature scientifique.

### Locus/disease/etchnic/other-specific database.
- Human Genome Variation Society .
- Leiden Open Variation Databse
- ==DECIPHER== : Base données de cytogénétique moléculaire pour les cliniciens et chercheurs reliant la génomique à la phénotype en se reposant sur le Ensembl genome Browser.


### Proposed Criteria for Interpretation of Sequence Variants

The following approach to evaluating evidence for a variant is intended for interpretation of variants observed in patients with suspected inherited (primarily mendelian) disorders in a clinical diagnostic laboratory setting. It is not intended for the interpretation of somatic variation, pharmacogenomic (PGx) variants, or variants in genes associated with multigenic non-mendelian complex disorders.

Each pathogenic criterion is weighted as very strong (PVS1), strong (PS1-4), moderate (PM1-6), or suporting (PP1-5) and each benign criterion is wieghted as stand-alone (BA1), strong (BS1-4), or supporting (BP1-6)

#### PVS1 null variants
Certain type of variants ( nonsense, frameshift, canonical +1 or 2 splice sites, initiation codon, single exon or multiexon deletion) can often be assumed to disrupt gene function by leading to a complete absence of the gene product by lack of transcription or nonsense-mediated decay of an altered transcript. One muste exercise caution when classifying these variants as pathogenic by consdering the following principles

(i) When classifying such variants as pathogenic ensure that null variant is a known mechanism of pathogenicity consistent with the established inheritance pattern for the disease.

(ii) One must also be cautious when interpreting truncating variants downstream of the most 3' truncating variant established as pathogenic in the literature.

(iii) For splice-site variants, the variant may lead to exon skipping, shortening, or inclusion of intronic material as a result of alternative donor/acceptor site usage or creation of new sites.
