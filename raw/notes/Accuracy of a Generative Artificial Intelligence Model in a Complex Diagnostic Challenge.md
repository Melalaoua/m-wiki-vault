```ad-abstract
Recent advances in artificial intelligence (AI) have led to generative models capable of accurate and detailed text-based responses to written prompts (“chats”). These models score highly on standardized medical examinations.[1](https://jamanetwork.com/journals/jama/fullarticle/2806457#jld230037r1) Less is known about their performance in clinical applications like complex diagnostic reasoning. We assessed the accuracy of one such model (Generative Pre-trained Transformer 4 [GPT-4]) in a series of diagnostically difficult cases.
```
