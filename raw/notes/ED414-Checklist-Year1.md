## 🔴 Immediate / At Start of PhD

- [x] Create and complete your **ADUM** account (national doctoral registration platform) — required for all enrollment and re-enrollment steps
- [x] Activate your **AMETHIS** account — used to log training hours and download training attestations
- [x] Complete **administrative registration** at Université de Strasbourg (deadline: **September 30**)
- [x] Sign the **Convention de Formation Doctorale** (Doctoral Training Agreement) with your thesis director — mandatory under Article 12 of the Decree of 25 May 2016; can be revised at each re-enrollment

## 🟠 Early Year 1 — Training Agreement (CIF)

The **Convention Individuelle de Formation (CIF)** defines the full training plan for the PhD. It is filled in at first registration (pre-populated from Apogée data), signed by the thesis director and the doctoral school, and **revised at each re-enrollment**. All training requests and validations go through **AMETHIS**.

### CIF — What it must cover (8 mandatory articles)

- [ ] **Article 1** — Full-time or part-time status (quotité de travail), and planned duration (3 years FT; 3–6 years PT)
- [ ] **Article 2** — Research calendar; supervision modalities (thesis director, co-directors, co-supervisors and their respective roles); material conditions and funding (operating costs, travel, training, congress, CSI logistics); integration into the lab
- [ ] **Article 3** — Publication policy: publication signature conventions (author order, institutional affiliations), confidentiality clauses, intellectual property rights
- [ ] **Article 4** — Professional project (provisional, revisable) and individual training plan (see training breakdown below)

> 💡 IP note: ideas, discoveries, scientific theories and mathematical methods are **not patentable** — only technical inventions are. Salaried doctoral candidates split IP 50/50 with employer.

### CIF — Training hours breakdown (108 h minimum over full PhD)

- [ ] Draft the **108-hour training plan** with thesis director, split as:
  - [ ] **54 h — Disciplinary training** (minimum **3 different themes or actions**):
    - Scientific events (congresses, thematic schools, workshops): **7 h/day** (flat rate, no bonus for presenting)
    - Master courses: max **10 h**, with signed attendance sheet + DT justification letter
    - Scientific MOOCs: max **10 h/MOOC**, max **27 h** total — certificate required
    - DU/DIU diplomas: max **30 h/diploma** (if domain matches ED414 scope)
    - Animal experimentation training — *concepteur* level: **30 h**
    - Experimental surgery training: **30 h**
    - ED414-organised courses (ImageJ, microscopy, biostatistics, etc.)
  - [ ] **54 h — Transferable/professional skills** (minimum **3 different themes**):
    - Ethics committee participation (e.g. CREMEAS): max **18 h** (= 1/3 of transferable hours), with proof
    - Scientific event organisation (Fête de la Science, retreats, congresses…): **4 h/full day**, **2 h/half day** — ⚠️ lab meetings and department meetings do **NOT** count
    - Transversal MOOCs: max **10 h/MOOC**, max **27 h** total — certificate required
    - Scientific vulgarisation presentations: max **2 h/event**
    - Other: IP & entrepreneurship, communication, career planning, FLE (French for non-native speakers), socio-economic openings…

> ⚠️ **Does NOT count toward the 108 h:**
> - MOOC *Intégrité dans les métiers de la recherche* (mandatory, but counted separately — no hours awarded)
> - Specialised software training (GraphPad, Icy…) outside a broader thematic course
> - Mandatory professional/regulatory training (clinical research, quality control…)
> - Lab internships in another research unit (counted as research experience, not training)

### CIF — Mandatory Year 1 items (required for re-enrollment in D2)

- [x] ✅ **Formation "Charte de déontologie des métiers de la recherche"** — mandatory for thesis defence (Art. 3 of Arrêté du 25 mai 2016)
- [ ] ✅ **MOOC Intégrité** — complete the MOOC and obtain the **certificate of success** (required for re-enrollment; does not count toward 108 h)
- [ ] ✅ **1 international congress** — with presentation of thesis work (oral or poster) — must be completed over the PhD (plan early)

> 🔹 **Special cases:**
> - **Co-tutelle**: 27 h disciplinary + 27 h transferable (half the usual quota), plus ethics + integrity + 1 congress as above
> - **Salaried part-time professionals** (doctors, psychologists, teachers…): exempt from the 54 h transferable hours (encouraged but not required); 54 h disciplinary + ethics + integrity remain mandatory

- [x] Identify and pre-enroll in training courses via **Espace Ernest** (the ED414 training catalogue): [ed.vie-sante.unistra.fr/en/training/espace-ernest/](http://ed.vie-sante.unistra.fr/en/training/espace-ernest/)

## 🟠 Early Year 1 — Thesis Follow-up Committee (CSI)

The **Comité de Suivi Individuel (CSI)** is mandatory from Year 1 (required to move from D1 to D2, since Jan 1 2023).

- [ ] Choose **2 CSI members** jointly with your thesis director:
  - Member 1: expert in the field of your PhD project
  - Member 2: experienced PhD supervisor (may or may not be a specialist in your field)
  - Neither can be involved in your PhD project
  - At least one must hold an **HDR** (Habilitation à Diriger des Recherches)
  - Can be from Université de Strasbourg or another French or foreign university
- [ ] Record CSI composition in your CIF — submitted to the doctoral school for validation
- [ ] **Schedule the CSI meeting** for **March–May** of Year 1

---

## 🟡 March–May Year 1 — CSI Annual Meeting

- [ ] Prepare a **scientific presentation** (talk + Q&A) for the CSI
- [ ] The CSI will assess:
  - Your takeover of the research project
  - Progress of work
  - Training conditions
  - (Discussion with you happens *without* the thesis director present for part of the meeting)
- [ ] After the meeting: ensure the **CSI report is submitted** to the ED414 — this report conditions re-enrollment in Year 2
- [ ] Deadline for CSI report reception by ED414: **July 10** (re-registration application on Amethis: **before August 31**)

---

## 🟡 May 2026 — DS Days (Annual PhD Event)

The **DS Days** is a 2-day annual seminar for all PhD students of ED414. Attendance is expected/encouraged.

- **Dates: May 6–7, 2026** · Strasbourg
- **What it is:** Research presentations (blitz talks, long talks, posters), career roundtables, women in science discussions, doctoral association forum. Top presentations receive prizes.
- [ ] **Register** at: [ed-days-2026.sciencesconf.org](https://ed-days-2026.sciencesconf.org/) (via "Inscription" page)
- [ ] **Submit an abstract** via "Mes dépôts" in your account (abstract deadline TBD)
- [ ] Choose your format: **Blitz talk** (3 min) · **Long talk** (10 min) · **Poster**

---

## 🟢 End of Year 1 — Re-enrollment (Réinscription)

To enroll in Year 2, all of the following must be completed:

- [ ] **CSI report received** by ED414 by July 10
- [ ] **Amethis readmission application** submitted before **August 31**
- [ ] Download your **training attestation** from Amethis and attach it to your re-enrollment file
- [ ] Log into ADUM → click "I wish to declare my re-enrolment in 2nd year of PhD" → follow the steps
- [ ] Complete **administrative registration** for Year 2 before **September 30**
- [ ] Check that all **mandatory Year 1 items** are validated in AMETHIS:
  - [ ] Charte de déontologie training ✅
  - [ ] MOOC Intégrité certificate ✅
  - [ ] 1 international congress (can be during Year 2–3 if not yet done)
